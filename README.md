## What is Ketamine?
Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic originally developed in the 1960s for medical use. In 2026, it remains a Schedule III controlled substance in the United States under the DEA, used primarily in veterinary and human medicine for sedation and pain management. Its unique properties have also led to off-label applications in treating depression and chronic pain.

### Medical Applications of Ketamine
- **Anesthesia**: Ketamine induces a trance-like state, making it ideal for short procedures, especially in emergency settings.
- **Mental Health Treatment**: Low-dose ketamine infusions show promise for treatment-resistant depression, with FDA-approved forms like esketamine (Spravato) available since 2019.
- **Veterinary Use**: Commonly administered to animals for sedation.

Research in 2026 continues to explore ketamine's role in neuroplasticity, potentially revolutionizing mental health care.

## Risks and Side Effects of Ketamine
While ketamine has therapeutic benefits, misuse can lead to serious health issues. Recreational use, often sought for its hallucinogenic effects, is highly dangerous.

### Common Side Effects
- Short-term: Nausea, dizziness, elevated blood pressure, and hallucinations.
- Long-term: Bladder damage (ketamine cystitis), cognitive impairments, and dependency.

Overdose risks include respiratory depression and psychosis. In 2026, public health campaigns emphasize harm reduction, urging users to seek professional help.

## Legality of Ketamine in 2026
Ketamine's legal status varies globally:
- **United States**: Prescription-only; illegal to buy or sell without authorization.
- **Europe**: Similar controls under the UN Convention on Psychotropic Substances.
- **Other Regions**: Some countries permit medical use but prohibit recreational possession.

Buying ketamine online or from unregulated sources is illegal and risky due to contamination and counterfeit products. Law enforcement actively monitors illicit trade.

2. Explore clinical trials via platforms like ClinicalTrials.gov.
3. For depression treatment, seek esketamine therapy through approved clinics.

Never attempt self-medication. Telehealth services in 2026 make legal access easier, but verification is key.

### Is ketamine addictive?
Yes, ketamine can lead to psychological dependence with repeated use, though physical addiction is less common than with opioids.

### Can I buy ketamine for personal use?
No, only through a prescription. Illicit purchases are illegal and unsafe.

### What are the alternatives to ketamine for depression?

### How has ketamine regulation changed by 2026?
